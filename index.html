Biography

Starting out in 2010 the main goal of the band was to write and play party oriented punk rock and just have fun with it. Founding the band on this philosophy the boys agreed upon the name The Friday Prophets. With the train was in motion the song-writing began, and the first song that was finished was baptized with the name Punk Rock Flu. A song that without a doubt lived up to the party punk building blocks, but as more songs came along the artistic and creative scope broadened. Fourteen songs later, the band had finished their first full-length album, Bits & Pieces which was released in Novemeber 2013. The album has the sound of the fast and melodic 90' punk rock era. In the era when Green released Dookie, NOFX put out Punk in Drublic and The Offspring dropped Smash. It's safe to say that these albums are all big influences and has helped shaped the bands creative output. Just like these bands mentioned the music is melody driven and the lyrics scrutinizes any aspect of life which seem important to write about at the moment. But the influences go way beyond music and can come in any shape and form, from everything from books, articles, poetry, science, personal relationships and a whole lot of self-reflection and more. The guys in the band loves playing shows, seeing new places and meeting new faces. And beer of course.
