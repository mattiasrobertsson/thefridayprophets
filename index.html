Biography

Starting out in 2010 the main goal of the band was to write and play party oriented punk rock and just have fun with it. Founding the band upon this philosophy the boys agreed on the name The Friday Prophets. With the train in motion the song-writing began, and the first song that came to form was titled Punk Rock Flu, a song that without a doubt expressed the bands party punk building blocks. As more songs came along the artistic and creative scope began broadening, and fourteen melodies later the first full-length album was done/finished. It was named Bits & Pieces and (was) released in November 2013. The album has the sound of the fast and melodic 90's punk rock era, when Green Day released Dookie, Bad Religion put out Stranger Than Fiction and The Offspring dropped Smash. It's safe to say that these albums mentioned definitely has been big influences and has helped shaped The Friday Prophets creative output. The influences however, goes way beyond that of music and the band takes their inspiration from everything from books, articles, poetry, science, personal relationships, self-reflection and more. A lot of emphasis is placed on writing purposeful lyricism, lyrics that scrutinizes both narrow and grand aspects of life, whatever the subject may concern. The Friday Prophets is currently working mostly with a/an? DIY approach and the guys aim is to get out and play shows, seeing new places and meeting new faces. If you want to get involved in anyway you'll reach the band on their social media platforms or at thefridayprophets@gmail.com.
